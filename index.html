FAQ

Your questions, answered clearly

How much does solar cost in Australia in 2026?
A quality 6.6kW system in Australia typically costs $4,500–$7,000 after federal STC rebates in 2026, depending on your state, panel brand, and installer. Adding a 10kWh battery typically adds $8,000–$14,000. Use our free matching service to get a personalised quote for your home.
Is solar worth it in Australia in 2026?
Absolutely. For most homeowners solar is one of the best financial decisions available. The average payback period on a quality 6.6kW system is 3–5 years, with annual savings of $1,500–$2,700 depending on usage and location. Panel warranties run 25 years — meaning 20+ years of effectively free electricity after payback.
What is a CEC-accredited solar installer?
A CEC (Clean Energy Council) accredited installer has completed approved training, complies with Australian installation standards, and is authorised to install systems qualifying for the federal solar rebate (STCs). You must use a CEC-accredited installer to access government rebates. Every installer in the ClearWatt network is CEC-accredited and fully insured.
Is this service really free? What's the catch?
Genuinely free. We may earn a referral fee from the installer if and when you choose to proceed with an installation — but only then, and it never affects your quoted price. The matching service is completely free with zero obligation, ever.
How are your installers vetted?
Every installer in our network must hold current CEC accreditation, carry appropriate insurance, maintain verifiable 4★+ customer reviews, and meet our quality standards. We audit the network regularly and remove underperformers immediately.
Will I be hassled by sales calls?
No. Your matched installer contacts you once to deliver a quote. If you're not interested, say so — our network guidelines require them to respect that immediately with no follow-ups. We take this seriously and will remove installers who don't comply.
